Tortang Talong

Tortang Talong

4/5

(3)

A classic filipino breakfast dish that is filled with nutrients and deliciousness. It is easy to make and only needs a few ingredients to make. A great option for college dormers to start up their day before going to school! This recipe is rich in vitamins C, K, and B6. Pair it with rice and you are good!

Recipe Info

Servings:

4 Servings

Cook Time:

12 Minutes

Total Time:

27 Minutes

Prep Time:

15 Minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces Chinese eggplant
  • 1 piece raw egg
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Preparation

Step 1 Remove the skin from the eggplant

Start out by taking your 4 pieces of Chinese eggplant and grilling them. Do this until the skin becomes nearly black in color, then let it cool. Once it is safe to touch, start peeling the skin off, and then set it aside.

Step 2 Create the egg coating for the eggplant

In a bowl, crack open 2 raw eggs. Sprinkle 1 teaspoon of salt for added flavor onto the bowl, and then beat the mixture. Now take out your skinless eggplant from earlier, and put it on a flat surface.

Step 3 Flatten the eggplant and coat it

Grab your fork and flatten the eggplants. And then get your bowl with the beaten eggs and salt. Making sure you've coated it fully, dip your flattened eggplant inside the bowl.

Step 4 Fry the Eggplants

Heat up a pan, and then add 4 tablespoons of cooking oil inside. Then place your coated eggplants on the pan, and fry these. Keep an eye on them, and ensure that you get both sides fried nicely. This should take approximately 3 to 4 minutes per side with medium heat.

Jenny Wilson

9/18/16

Tortang talong reminds me of home. The smoky flavor of the grilled eggplant combined with the soft, fluffy egg coating is simply comforting. My mom used to make this with a bit of ground pork mixed in, and it was always a favorite during lunch. Paired with garlic rice and a bit of ketchup or vinegar, it's a simple yet satisfying meal. If you're looking for a budget-friendly, delicious Filipino dish, this is it!

Esther Howard

5/27/15

As someone who loves plant-based meals, I really enjoyed tortang talong. It has a wonderful balance of textures—the slightly crisp egg coating contrasts nicely with the soft, smoky eggplant inside. Plus, it's high in fiber and packed with nutrients. I personally like adding chopped onions and a bit of cheese for extra flavor. The only downside is that it absorbs oil quickly, so pan-frying with minimal oil is a must. Overall, a great meat-free dish that's both delicious and nutritious!

Wade Warren

10/6/13

I was excited to try tortang talong, but I found it a bit too plain for my taste. While the eggplant's smokiness was nice, the dish itself felt like it needed more seasoning. I tried it with ketchup, soy sauce, and vinegar, which helped, but on its own, it was underwhelming. Maybe adding garlic, onions, or some meat would improve the flavor. It's a decent side dish, but not something I’d crave regularly.
